# ACUSTICA – GLOSSARY AND SYMBOLS

# ───────────────────────────────────────────────
# ACOUSTICS AND PHYSICS TERMS
# ───────────────────────────────────────────────
soundboard = main vibrating plate (top) of the guitar body that radiates most of the sound energy
back_plate = lower plate of the guitar body, dynamically coupled with the top and air cavity
bridge_torque = rotational moment acting on the bridge due to string tension and break angle
modal_frequency = natural resonance frequency of a vibration mode
mode_shape = spatial displacement pattern of the plate at a given resonance
resonance = frequency where a system exhibits maximum vibration amplitude
Q_factor = measure of damping, ratio of stored to dissipated energy per cycle
impedance = mechanical opposition to motion, ratio of force to velocity at a point
mobility = mechanical admittance, inverse of impedance
compliance = inverse of stiffness, displacement per unit force
modal_coupling = energy exchange between different resonant subsystems (top, air, back, strings)
Helmholtz_resonance = air cavity resonance governed by cavity volume, soundhole size, and coupling with other modes.
stiffness = resistance of a structure to deformation
mass_loading = added mass influencing modal frequencies and amplitudes
damping = energy loss through internal friction or radiation
phase_response = timing relationship between different frequency components
transfer_function = ratio of output to input response across frequency
accelerance = acceleration per unit applied force, measured in FRF testing
impulse_response = system’s time-domain reaction to a short excitation
FRF = Frequency Response Function, amplitude and phase vs frequency
eigenmode = vibration pattern from modal analysis or FEM computation
orthotropy = directional dependence of mechanical properties with three perpendicular axes
anisotropy = non-uniform mechanical behavior with respect to direction

# ───────────────────────────────────────────────
# MATERIAL AND STRUCTURAL TERMS
# ───────────────────────────────────────────────
tonewood = wood species selected for instrument making, characterized by stiffness, density, and damping
density_rho = mass per unit volume, expressed in kg/m³
E_long = longitudinal Young’s modulus, parallel to grain
E_cross = cross-grain Young’s modulus
G_shear = shear modulus, coupling angular deformation to shear stress
tan_delta = loss tangent, dimensionless damping coefficient
Q = quality factor, inverse of 2π times tan_delta
EI = flexural rigidity, product of elastic modulus and second moment of area
TPC = Tonewood Parameters Characterization, proprietary system for measuring tonewood properties
moisture_content = percentage of water in wood affecting stiffness and damping
deflection = displacement of a structure under load
neutral_axis = axis along which no strain occurs during bending
bending_stiffness = product EI defining resistance to curvature
elastic_modulus = intrinsic material stiffness coefficient
Poisson_ratio = ratio of lateral to longitudinal strain
longitudinal_wave_velocity = sqrt(E_long / ρ), important for radiation efficiency
shear_wave_velocity = sqrt(G_shear / ρ)
orthotropic_plate = plate whose material properties differ along grain directions
bracing = system of reinforcing beams that defines stiffness distribution of the soundboard

# ───────────────────────────────────────────────
# GUITAR DESIGN AND MEASUREMENT TERMS
# ───────────────────────────────────────────────
bridge_force = total string load transmitted through the bridge to the top
break_angle = angle formed by the string over the saddle; influences downforce and torque
saddle_height = distance from soundboard to top of saddle
bridge_rotation = angular deformation of the bridge under string tension
bracing_pattern = layout of braces (e.g., X, fan, lattice) shaping stiffness anisotropy
modal_shapes = spatial vibration patterns corresponding to resonant frequencies
frequency_response = amplitude of vibration vs excitation frequency
FEM = Finite Element Method, numerical approach to structural analysis
FEM_validation = experimental measurement verifying FEM-predicted modal frequencies
4DOF_model = analytical model describing coupled dynamics of top, back, air, and bridge masses
monopole_mode = first top mode radiating sound, the center of tonal balance
dipole_mode = opposite-phase regions on the top, influencing tone color
tripole_mode = higher-order resonance mode with three main antinodes
air_mode = first resonance of enclosed air cavity, typically 90–120 Hz
bridge_mass_effect = influence of bridge weight on bandwidth extension at higher frequencies
modal_target = desired resonance frequency used for tuning and optimization
plate_coupling = dynamic interaction between plates through air or structural contact
modal_mobility = vibration velocity per unit force at a given frequency
radiation_coefficient = E/ρ ratio determining efficiency of sound radiation
impedance_matching = condition of optimal energy transfer between string and structure

# ───────────────────────────────────────────────
# TUNING, OPTIMIZATION, AND MEASUREMENT ROUTINES
# ───────────────────────────────────────────────
tuning_step = single operation altering stiffness, mass, or damping to approach target frequency
mass_reduction = controlled thinning or removal of material to raise mobility
stiffness_reduction = mechanical adjustment lowering resonance frequency
modal_alignment = tuning top and back modes to defined frequency ratio (e.g., T(1,1)₁ vs T(1,1)₂)
frequency_shift = change in modal frequency due to structural modification
mobility_measurement = evaluation of deflection and mass of a specific mode
response_curve = measured or simulated FRF showing amplitude vs frequency
impedance_measurement = determination of input impedance using accelerometer and force sensor
acoustic_coupling = mutual influence between modes of different parts
Q_measurement = evaluation of damping factor from FRF width or TPC analysis
optimization_loop = iterative process adjusting design parameters to meet acoustic goals

# ───────────────────────────────────────────────
# DATA, SIMULATION, AND ANALYSIS TERMS
# ───────────────────────────────────────────────
dataset = structured table of numerical or categorical data used for learning or reference
parameter_sweep = systematic variation of model parameters to explore sensitivity
simulation_model = computational representation of acoustic system behavior
eigenfrequency_analysis = FEM computation of mode frequencies
mesh_density = discretization parameter in FEM defining element size
boundary_condition = constraint defining motion limits in simulation
isotropic_model = simplification assuming uniform material properties
orthotropic_model = realistic model including wood anisotropy
validation_dataset = experimental data set used to verify simulation accuracy
sensitivity_analysis = quantification of model output dependence on parameters
calibration = adjustment of model parameters to align with measurements
error_metric = numerical indicator of prediction accuracy (e.g., RMS error)
frequency_window = spectral range considered in analysis
log_scale = logarithmic axis used for wide frequency representation

# ───────────────────────────────────────────────
# PERCEPTUAL AND DESIGN LANGUAGE
# ───────────────────────────────────────────────
timbre = perceived tonal quality determined by spectral content and temporal evolution
projection = ability of the instrument to radiate sound efficiently at distance
balance = evenness of energy distribution across the spectrum
responsiveness = speed and sensitivity of the instrument to excitation
clarity = perceptual separation of partials and transients
warmth = tonal emphasis toward lower frequencies
brightness = tonal emphasis toward higher frequencies
loudness = perceived sound power related to radiated acoustic pressure
dynamics = range between softest and loudest possible sound
sustain = duration of vibration before amplitude decays
coloration = deviation from flat frequency response
homogeneity = uniform response across strings and positions

# ───────────────────────────────────────────────
# MODAL NOMENCLATURE AND BODY RESONANCES
# ───────────────────────────────────────────────
T(1,1)₁ = air mode, first acoustic resonance of the entire cavity breathing motion through soundhole; 90–110 Hz 
T(1,1)₂ = soundboard monopole, single antinode resonance; 170–220 Hz 
T(1,1)₃ = back monopole, single antinode resonance; 200–240 Hz 
T(2,1) = cross dipole, double antiphase antinodes transversal resonance; 250-400 Hz
T(1,2) = long dipole, double antiphase antinodes longitudinal resonance; 250-400 Hz
T(3,1) = cross tripole, triple antiphase antinodes transversal resonance; 400-600 Hz
T(1,3) = long tripole, triple antiphase antinodes longitudinal resonance; 400-600 Hz

# ───────────────────────────────────────────────
# SYMBOLS AND FORMULAS
# ───────────────────────────────────────────────
# Basic Mechanics
F = m · a
σ = F / A
ε = ΔL / L₀
E = σ / ε
G = τ / γ
ρ = m / V
v_long = √(E / ρ)
v_shear = √(G / ρ)

# Vibration and Resonance
f₀ = (1 / 2π) · √(k / m)
Q = f₀ / Δf
ζ = 1 / (2Q)
A(ω) = F / √((k − mω²)² + (cω)²)

# Guitar-Specific Parameters
F_v = T · sin(α)
F_h = T · cos(α)
α = atan(D₃ / D₂)
τ_bridge = F_v · d
f_H = (c_acoustic / 2π) · √(A / (V · L))

# Monopole & Tone Profiler
f_s = (1 / 2π) · √(k_eq / m_eq)
Mobility = 1 / √(k_eq · m_eq)
WolfNote ≈ f_s proximity to string resonance

# Modal Quantities (4-DOF Simulator)
f_mode = (1 / 2π) · √(k_eff / m_eff)
η = c_eff / (2√(k_eff · m_eff))
|K − ω²M| = 0
Modal_coupling = Δf ∝ √(k_couple / (m₁ m₂))

# Flexural and Plate Theory (EI Tool)
I = (b · h³) / 12
EI = E · I
EI_total = EI_plate + Σ(EI_brace · area_fraction)
w(x) = (F x² / 6EI) · (3L − x)
f_plate ∝ (h / L²) · √(E / ρ)

# Coupled System Models
4DOF model → { top, back, air, sides } masses coupled by k_ij
|K − ω²M| = 0 → eigenfrequencies
Coupling stiffness k_ij and damping c_ij control modal interaction

# Acoustic Quantities
Z = F / v
Y = 1 / Z
Radiation_ratio = E / ρ
SPL = 20 · log₁₀(p / p_ref)
